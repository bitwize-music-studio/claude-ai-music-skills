# Interview and Biographical Sources Guide

Find first-hand accounts, interviews, and personal details for subjects.

---

## Finding Interviews

### Video Interviews

**YouTube**:
```
"[name]" interview
"[name]" keynote OR conference
"[name]" podcast
```

**Conference archives**:
- TED: ted.com
- SXSW: schedule.sxsw.com
- Tech conferences: Often post to YouTube

### Audio Interviews

**Podcast search**:
- Listen Notes: listennotes.com
- Apple Podcasts search
- Spotify search

**Common interview podcasts**:
- How I Built This (entrepreneurs)
- Lex Fridman Podcast (tech)
- Conan O'Brien Needs a Friend (celebrities)
- WTF with Marc Maron (varied)

### Print Interviews

**Search patterns**:
```
"[name]" "in an interview"
"[name]" "told me"
"[name]" profile site:newyorker.com
"[name]" Q&A OR "interview with"
```

**Long-form outlets**:
- New Yorker profiles
- Vanity Fair
- Wired
- New York Times Magazine

## Social Media Research

### Twitter/X
**Profile**: twitter.com/[username]
**Search**: `from:[username] [keyword]`
**Archive**: web.archive.org/web/*/twitter.com/[username]

What to find:
- Personal opinions
- Reactions to events
- Interactions with others
- Self-presentation

### LinkedIn
- Career timeline
- Education history
- Professional connections
- Posts/articles

### Other Platforms
- Facebook (increasingly private)
- Instagram (visual personality)
- Reddit (tech figures often active)
- Hacker News (tech community)

### Archiving Social Media
Accounts get deleted. Archive immediately:
1. Archive.org: save URLs
2. Archive.today: create snapshots
3. Screenshots: critical posts

## Profile Building

### Core Questions
1. **Origin**: Where from, family background, class
2. **Formation**: Education, early jobs, mentors
3. **Motivation**: Why did they do what they did?
4. **Method**: How did they operate?
5. **Relationships**: Key people in their life
6. **Turning points**: Moments that changed their path
7. **Contradictions**: What complicates the narrative?

### Information Sources by Type

| Info Type | Best Sources |
|-----------|-------------|
| Birthplace/date | Wikipedia, LinkedIn, interviews |
| Education | LinkedIn, profiles, interviews |
| Career path | LinkedIn, news coverage |
| Personal life | Profiles, interviews, social media |
| Personality | Interviews, colleague quotes |
| Motivations | First-hand interviews, speeches |

## Book Research

### Finding Books
- Amazon: Search subject's name
- Google Books: Preview passages
- Library databases: Full access
- Goodreads: Reviews may have quotes

### Types of Useful Books
- **Biography**: By journalist/historian
- **Autobiography**: Subject's own words
- **Industry history**: Mentions subject
- **Colleague memoir**: Different perspective

### Extracting from Books
- Use Google Books preview for key passages
- Check book index for relevant pages
- Read reviews for key revelations
- Library ILL for full access

## First-Hand Account Types

### Direct Sources (Preferred)
- Interviews they gave
- Speeches/talks
- Published writings
- Social media posts
- Court testimony

### Second-Hand Sources
- Profiles quoting them
- Colleague interviews
- Biographies
- Documentary appearances

### Verification
For critical quotes:
1. Find primary source if possible
2. Cross-reference multiple outlets
3. Check for corrections/denials
4. Note context and date

## Character Development

### Finding Humanity
Look for:
- **Hobbies/interests**: What do they do outside work?
- **Habits/quirks**: How do they present themselves?
- **Relationships**: Who matters to them?
- **Failures**: What went wrong?
- **Growth**: How did they change?

### Searching for Details
```
"[name]" hobby OR "in his spare time"
"[name]" wife OR husband OR family
"[name]" "grew up" OR childhood
"[name]" college OR university OR "dropped out"
"[name]" "friends say" OR "colleagues describe"
```

## Ethical Considerations

### Public vs. Private
**Fair game**:
- Public statements
- Professional history
- Published interviews
- Public social media

**Use carefully**:
- Family members (especially children)
- Health information
- Financial difficulties
- Personal relationships

### Living vs. Deceased
**Living subjects**:
- May respond to the work
- Defamation concerns
- Current context matters

**Deceased subjects**:
- Consider family impact
- Legacy is contested
- Death circumstances may be sensitive

## For Lyrics

### Quote Types
**Direct (documented)**:
> He told the Times, "I never saw a dime"

**Paraphrased (based on reporting)**:
> He claimed he didn't know

**Narrator summary (based on sources)**:
> The evidence mounted, day by day

### Character Details
Best details for lyrics:
- Specific facts ("dropped out after one semester")
- Contradictions (public image vs. reality)
- Pivotal moments (decisions that changed everything)
- Verbal tics (phrases they repeat)
