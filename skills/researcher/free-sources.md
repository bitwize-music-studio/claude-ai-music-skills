# Free Document Sources

This reference lists free sources for court documents and legal materials.

## Key Principle

U.S. federal court documents have **no copyright** (17 U.S.C. ยง 105). PACER charges for *access convenience*, not ownership. These documents are often freely available from third-party archives.

**ALWAYS exhaust free sources before considering PACER.**

---

## Free Source Directory

### 1. DocumentCloud
**URL**: https://www.documentcloud.org/

- Nonprofit document archive
- Journalists/researchers upload PACER docs after purchasing
- Search by case name, party names, or keywords
- Fully free, no account needed

### 2. CourtListener / RECAP Project
**URL**: https://www.courtlistener.com/

- Crowdsourced PACER document archive
- Browser extension uploads docs automatically when users view them
- Search by case number, docket, or party name
- **Install RECAP extension**: https://free.law/recap/
- Covers millions of PACER documents

### 3. Scribd
**URL**: https://www.scribd.com/

- User-uploaded document sharing
- Many journalists upload court docs after obtaining them
- Search: "[Case name] indictment" or "[Party name] complaint"
- Free to view (may require free account)

### 4. Justia
**URL**: https://www.justia.com/

- Free legal database
- Appellate opinions fully available
- Some district court opinions and key documents
- Search by case number or party names

### 5. Court Websites Directly

- Some courts post high-profile cases on their websites
- Example: Northern District of California "Cases of Interest"
- Search: "[Court name] cases of interest"

### 6. Government Agency Websites

**DOJ**: Press releases link to indictments/complaints
- https://www.justice.gov/usao/[district]/press-releases
- Direct PDF downloads often available

**SEC**: EDGAR database + litigation releases
- https://www.sec.gov/litigation
- Complaints and settlements publicly available

### 7. Legal Publisher Websites

- **Wolters Kluwer / CCH**: business.cch.com hosts some case docs
- **Bloomberg Law**: Some free access to public filings
- **Law360**: Trial coverage sometimes includes embedded docs
- Search by case name + "PDF"

### 8. News Organization Archives

- Major investigations often embed source documents
- ProPublica, New York Times, Wall Street Journal
- Check article footnotes/source links
- Some upload to DocumentCloud and link from articles

### 9. Academic/Research Sites

- **SSRN** (papers.ssrn.com): Legal scholarship with case doc appendices
- **University repositories**: Law schools archive major cases
- **Archive.org**: Internet Archive sometimes hosts court docs

### 10. Specialty Legal Sites

- **PlainSite** (https://www.plainsite.org/): Free PACER alternative
- **Docket Alarm**: Limited free access to dockets
- **Lex Machina**: Some free trial access for legal analytics

---

## Search Strategy

For any case, search in this order:

1. **DocumentCloud** - Most comprehensive for recent cases
2. **CourtListener** - Great for federal cases with RECAP coverage
3. **Scribd** - Good for documents journalists have uploaded
4. **Direct court/agency sites** - For high-profile cases
5. **News archives** - For embedded documents in investigations

For automated searching across all sources, use `/document-hunter`.
