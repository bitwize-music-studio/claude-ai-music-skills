# Mest — Deep Dive

## Overview

Mest is an American pop-punk band formed in October 1995 in Blue Island, Illinois — a small working-class town on the south side of Chicago. The band was founded by cousins Tony Lovato (vocals, guitar) and Matt Lovato (bass), who grew up across the street from each other and started playing together at age seven, borrowing instruments from Tony's father. Tony came up with the band name while looking at a case of Milwaukee's Best beer.

Mest rose to prominence in the early 2000s pop-punk wave alongside Good Charlotte, Sum 41, Simple Plan, and New Found Glory. Signed to Maverick Records through a connection with Goldfinger's John Feldmann — who produced all four of their major-label albums — Mest became Warped Tour regulars and built a loyal fanbase through relentless touring and energetic live shows. Their biggest commercial moment came with the 2003 single "Jaded (These Years)" featuring Benji Madden of Good Charlotte, which helped their self-titled album reach #64 on the Billboard 200.

Despite never achieving the mainstream crossover of peers like Blink-182 or Good Charlotte, Mest carved out a distinct identity in the pop-punk scene: Chicago-suburb kids playing SoCal-style pop-punk with genuine working-class grit. Their lyrics centered on suburban boredom, growing up, broken relationships, and the desire to escape — themes delivered with an earnest, unpolished energy that connected deeply with their audience.

The band broke up in 2006, reunited in various configurations starting in 2008, and fully reunited with the classic lineup in 2018 to record *Masquerade* (2020). They remain active, releasing *Youth* in 2024 via SBAM Records.

## Members

### Tony Lovato — Lead Vocals, Guitar (1995–2006, 2008–present)

- **Role:** Co-founder, primary songwriter, frontman, and the only constant member across all eras of Mest
- **Born and raised:** Blue Island, Illinois (south side of Chicago)
- **Guitar gear:** Gibson SG Special, Gibson Les Paul Goldtop, Epiphone Les Paul '50s Goldtop
- **Vocal style:** Nasal, slightly raspy pop-punk delivery with an everyman quality. Not technically polished — more raw and conversational, closer to the skate punk vocal tradition than the cleaner pop-punk crooning of bands like New Found Glory. Carries a natural urgency that sells the lyrical themes of frustration and restlessness.
- **Songwriting:** Writes virtually all Mest material. Lyrics focus on suburban ennui, relationship struggles, growing up, and the tension between wanting to escape and being stuck.
- **Feldmann connection:** Tony's letter to Goldfinger's John Feldmann was the catalyst for Mest's career — Feldmann got them signed to Maverick and produced their first four major-label albums.
- **Side project:** Formed Kisses for Kings with Mike Longworth and Richie Gonzales during Mest's hiatus.
- **Health:** Required back surgery during the Mest years, exacerbated by relentless touring.

### Matt Lovato — Bass (1995–2006, 2015–present)

- **Role:** Co-founder, Tony's cousin
- **Background:** Grew up across the street from Tony in Blue Island. Started playing bass at age seven alongside Tony on guitar.
- **Post-breakup project:** Formed The Twilight and the Sound with Jeremiah Rangel, announced via Mest's MySpace page on October 27, 2008.
- **Reunion:** Did not participate in Tony's 2008 reunion attempt but returned for the classic lineup reunion in 2015 and again in 2018 for the *Masquerade* sessions.

### Jeremiah Rangel — Lead Guitar, Vocals (1998–2006, 2015–present)

- **Joined:** 1998, replacing Steve Lovato (Tony's brother)
- **Role:** Lead guitar, backing vocals. Provides the melodic guitar lines and leads that distinguish Mest's sound from purely rhythm-driven pop-punk.
- **Post-breakup project:** The Twilight and the Sound with Matt Lovato.
- **Reunion:** Returned for the classic lineup reunion in 2015 and the *Masquerade* sessions in 2018.

### Nick Gigler — Drums (1997–2006, 2015–present)

- **Joined:** 1997
- **Drumming style:** Driving, energetic pop-punk drumming with fills that push the tempo. Straightforward but effective — keeps the energy high without overplaying.
- **Health:** Required surgery for carpal tunnel syndrome in 2005, cutting short a tour with Social Distortion.
- **Post-breakup project:** Formed The Get Go (with members of Allister and Showoff) in January 2007. Also played in The A-Gang.
- **2008 tension:** Did not participate in Tony's 2008 reunion. He and Tony were not on speaking terms after the breakup. Confirmed that Matt and Jeremiah also declined to appear.
- **Reunion:** Returned for the classic lineup reunion in 2015 and the *Masquerade* sessions in 2018.

### Previous Members

- **Steve Lovato** — Guitar (1995–1998/1999). Tony's brother. Original guitarist before Jeremiah Rangel. Returned briefly during Tony's 2008 reunion tour when the other members declined to participate.
- **Mike Longworth** — Guitar (2008–2015). Played with Tony during the post-breakup era of Mest. Also in Kisses for Kings.
- **Richie Gonzales** — Drums (2010–2015). Replaced Nick Gigler during the non-classic-lineup era. Also in Kisses for Kings.
- **Casey Artus** — Bass (2020–2023). Filled in during a period when Matt Lovato was not touring.

## Albums

### Mo' Money, Mo' 40z (1998)

- **Label:** Self-released
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **Sound:** Raw, DIY pop-punk. Demo-quality production but showing the core Mest sound already forming — fast tempos, catchy hooks, suburban frustration.
- **Significance:** The self-released album that caught John Feldmann's attention and led to the Maverick Records deal.

### Wasting Time (2000)

- **Released:** July 2000
- **Label:** Maverick Records
- **Producer:** John Feldmann (Goldfinger)
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **Sound:** Polished pop-punk debut with Feldmann's production bringing clarity and punch to the band's raw energy. Fast, guitar-driven songs with catchy choruses. The production strikes a balance between letting instruments dominate and maintaining accessibility.
- **Key tracks:** "What's the Dillio?", "Drawing Board"
- **Significance:** Major-label debut. Established Mest as part of the Warped Tour pop-punk wave. Feldmann's production gave them a SoCal pop-punk sound despite their Chicago origins.

### Destination Unknown (2001)

- **Released:** October 2001
- **Label:** Maverick Records
- **Producer:** John Feldmann
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **Sound:** Refined and more confident version of the debut's formula. Tighter songwriting, bigger hooks, more dynamic arrangements. Emo-pop sensibilities blending with energetic guitar-and-drum-driven instrumentals.
- **Key tracks:** "Cadillac," "Fuct Up Kid," "Drawing Board," "Yesterday," "Mother's Prayer"
- **Chart:** #12 on Billboard Heatseekers
- **Significance:** Solidified their fanbase. "Cadillac" became a live staple and fan favorite. Supported by extensive touring including dates opening for Goldfinger. Went gold in Japan.

### Mest (Self-Titled) (2003)

- **Released:** June 10, 2003
- **Label:** Maverick Records
- **Producer:** John Feldmann
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **Sound:** Their most polished and commercially focused album. Pop-punk with occasional emo-tinged moments. Bigger production, more melodic hooks, a conscious push toward accessibility while retaining punk energy.
- **Key tracks:** "Jaded (These Years)" (feat. Benji Madden of Good Charlotte), "Rooftops," "Until I Met You," "Burning Bridges," "Shell Of Myself"
- **Chart:** #64 on Billboard 200
- **Significance:** Commercial peak. "Jaded (These Years)" was their biggest single, benefiting from the Good Charlotte crossover and MTV2 rotation. Followed by headlining Warped Tour dates in 2003. Went gold in Japan.

### Photographs (2005)

- **Released:** October 12, 2005
- **Label:** Maverick Records
- **Producer:** John Feldmann
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **12 tracks, 43 minutes**
- **Sound:** More mature, slightly darker tone. Still pop-punk at its core but with more emotional depth and introspective lyrics. Some songs pushed toward a more alt-rock direction.
- **Key tracks:** "Kiss Me, Kill Me," "Take Me Away (Cried Out to Heaven)"
- **Bonus:** Limited copies included a bonus DVD titled *Seven Deadly Sins*
- **Significance:** Final Maverick Records album. Final album before the breakup. The band was exhausted from years of touring, and Tony Lovato was dealing with back surgery. Headlined Warped Tour 2005. The band broke up the following year after eleven years together.

### Not What You Expected (2013/2014)

- **Released:** January 2014 (some sources list 2013)
- **Label:** Dead End Records (independent)
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato with non-classic lineup (Mike Longworth, Richie Gonzales)
- **11 tracks, 42 minutes**
- **Sound:** Return to form after eight years. Pop-punk with a slightly more mature edge, reflecting Tony's growth during the hiatus.
- **Significance:** First album in eight years, but only Tony from the classic lineup. A stepping stone toward the eventual full reunion.

### Broken Down (2014)

- **Label:** Self-released (Kickstarter-funded)
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato (solo, vocals and acoustic guitar)
- **Sound:** Acoustic reworkings of songs from across Mest's discography. Stripped down to just Tony's vocals and acoustic guitar.
- **Notes:** Kickstarter launched November 13, 2013, successfully funded December 11. Does not include any songs from *Not What You Expected*.
- **Significance:** Demonstrated that Mest songs could work outside the pop-punk framework. Fan-funded release.

### Masquerade (2020)

- **Released:** January 17, 2020
- **Label:** Cyber Tracks (El Hefe of NOFX's label)
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler (classic lineup)
- **12 tracks, 42:13**
- **Sound:** Classic Mest pop-punk with updated production. The reunion album fans had been waiting for — all four original members together again for the first time since 2005.
- **Key tracks:** "Masquerade," "Dead End Street," "Don't Worry Son," "The Silence Left Behind"
- **Significance:** First album with the classic lineup since *Photographs* (2005). Released on NOFX guitarist El Hefe's Cyber Tracks label, connecting Mest to the Fat Wreck Chords / skate punk ecosystem. Announced October 2018 when the band signed with the label.

### Youth (2024)

- **Released:** June 21, 2024
- **Label:** SBAM Records
- **Lineup:** Tony Lovato, Matt Lovato, Jeremiah Rangel, Nick Gigler
- **7 tracks, 22 minutes**
- **Sound:** Nostalgic pop-punk revisiting the band's signature themes of youth and growing up. Guest features add variety while keeping the core Mest sound intact.
- **Key tracks:** "When We Were Young" (feat. Jaret Reddick of Bowling for Soup), "Hate You Sober" (feat. Spencer Charnas of Ice Nine Kills)
- **Significance:** First of three planned albums the band had been working on. Continued collaboration with notable pop-punk and adjacent artists. Self-released via Austrian punk label SBAM Records.

## Musical Analysis — What Makes Mest Sound Like Mest

### Guitar

Tony Lovato plays rhythm guitar (Gibson SG Special, Gibson/Epiphone Les Paul Goldtop) with a thick, punchy pop-punk tone — crunchy power chords, palm-muted verses opening into ringing open chords on choruses. Jeremiah Rangel handles lead guitar with melodic lines and occasional solos that stay hooky rather than flashy. The dual guitar approach gives Mest a fuller sound than many pop-punk acts: Lovato drives the rhythm while Rangel adds melodic texture on top. The guitar tone sits in the SoCal pop-punk tradition — distorted but clean enough to let the melodies shine through.

### Bass

Matt Lovato's bass follows the punk tradition of locking in with the guitar rhythm, providing the low-end foundation without straying far from the root notes. Punchy and present in the mix but not a lead instrument. Functional rather than melodic — it's the glue holding the rhythm section together.

### Drums

Nick Gigler plays driving, energetic pop-punk drums. Fast fills, steady kick patterns, crash-heavy accents on chorus entries. Not overly technical but tight and propulsive — he keeps the energy high and the tempos pushing forward. His drumming fits the skate punk-adjacent approach: reliable velocity with enough punch to fuel the band's live energy.

### Vocals

Tony Lovato's voice is Mest's most distinctive element. A nasal, slightly raspy delivery with an everyman quality — he sounds like the kid from the suburbs singing about wanting to get out, because he was. Not a trained singer; the rough edges are part of the appeal. He can shift from bratty pop-punk sneer to more earnest, emo-inflected emotion without losing authenticity. The vocal tone sits between Blink-182's Tom DeLonge (nasal, casual) and Green Day's Billie Joe Armstrong (slightly more melodic, more urgency).

### Production (John Feldmann Era)

John Feldmann produced all four Maverick-era albums, giving Mest a consistent sonic identity. His production style emphasizes clarity without overpolishing — guitars are crunchy and present, drums snap, vocals sit right on top. The mix strikes a balance between letting instruments dominate and maintaining radio accessibility. Feldmann's experience with Goldfinger and later with The Used and Blink-182 gave Mest a professional SoCal pop-punk sheen even though they came from working-class Chicago.

### Lyrical Themes

Mest's lyrics revolve around a tight cluster of related themes:
- **Suburban boredom and escape:** The desire to break free from small-town stagnation. Blue Island's working-class environment permeates the writing.
- **Growing up and nostalgia:** Tracks about youth slipping away, friends drifting apart, the passage of time.
- **Relationship frustration:** Breakups, miscommunication, longing. The emotional core of most Mest songs.
- **Self-doubt and frustration:** Feeling lost, confused, and directionless — captured in titles like "Shell Of Myself," "Lost, Broken, Confused," and "Fuct Up Kid."
- **Friendship and loyalty:** The band-of-brothers mentality. Songs about sticking together, shared experiences.

The lyrics are direct and unpretentious — no literary ambition, just honest emotional expression from a working-class perspective.

### Cited Influences

Green Day, Blink-182, Goldfinger, NOFX, The Offspring, Social Distortion, Descendents.

## Key Relationships

### John Feldmann (Goldfinger)

The most important figure in Mest's career outside the band itself. Tony Lovato wrote him a letter, Feldmann responded, let Mest open for Goldfinger, got them signed to Maverick Records, and produced all four of their major-label albums. Feldmann went on to become one of pop-punk's most prolific producers (The Used, Blink-182's *California*, 5 Seconds of Summer, Story of the Year).

### Good Charlotte / Benji Madden

Mest and Good Charlotte were close friends and frequent touring partners during the early 2000s pop-punk peak. Benji Madden's guest vocal on "Jaded (These Years)" was Mest's biggest commercial moment. Good Charlotte started as an opening act for Mest before both bands rose together. The relationship connected Mest to the MTV-era pop-punk mainstream.

### El Hefe (NOFX) / Cyber Tracks

El Hefe signed Mest to his Cyber Tracks label for the *Masquerade* album (2020), connecting the band to the independent punk infrastructure of the Fat Wreck Chords ecosystem.

### Warped Tour

Mest were Warped Tour regulars throughout the early 2000s, headlining in 2003 and 2005. The Warped Tour circuit was central to building their fanbase and connecting them to the broader pop-punk scene.

## Legacy and Influence

**Scene position:** Mest occupied a specific niche in the early 2000s pop-punk hierarchy — bigger than most underground bands, smaller than the MTV headliners. They were the band your friend's band wanted to be. Not the stadium act, but the Warped Tour lifers who earned their audience through grinding tour schedules and genuine connection with fans.

**Chicago punk representation:** One of the few pop-punk bands from the Chicago area to break through nationally in an era dominated by SoCal and East Coast acts. Proved that the SoCal pop-punk sound could come from the Midwest.

**Longevity:** Despite breakups, lineup changes, and a shifting industry, Mest's classic lineup reunited and continues to release music. The 2020 *Masquerade* and 2024 *Youth* albums demonstrate a band that still has creative drive.

**Warped Tour generation:** Part of the wave that defined pop-punk for kids who came of age between 2000 and 2005, alongside Good Charlotte, Sum 41, Simple Plan, New Found Glory, and Yellowcard.

## Reference Tracks (Mest Essential Listening)

### Defining the Sound
- **"Cadillac"** (Destination Unknown, 2001) — The quintessential Mest track. Fast, catchy, energetic — everything that makes Mest work in three minutes. Fan favorite and live staple.
- **"Drawing Board"** (Destination Unknown, 2001) — Frantic beat, urgent vocals, pure pop-punk adrenaline. One of their earliest fan favorites from the Maverick era.
- **"Jaded (These Years)"** (Mest, 2003) — Their biggest single, featuring Benji Madden of Good Charlotte. Represents Mest at their most polished and commercially accessible. The Good Charlotte connection captured perfectly.
- **"Rooftops"** (Mest, 2003) — Anthemic pop-punk with an emotional hook. Demonstrates Mest's ability to write songs that feel bigger than the band.

### Early/Raw
- **"What's the Dillio?"** (Wasting Time, 2000) — First major-label single. Raw pop-punk energy with Feldmann's production polish. Captures the debut-album excitement.
- **"Fuct Up Kid"** (Destination Unknown, 2001) — Hard-hitting, punk-leaning. Shows the grittier side of Mest's sound, closer to skate punk than pop-punk.

### Emotional/Mature
- **"Kiss Me, Kill Me"** (Photographs, 2005) — More mature, slightly darker pop-punk. Represents the band's evolution toward emotional depth on their final Maverick album.
- **"Take Me Away (Cried Out to Heaven)"** (Photographs, 2005) — Introspective and yearning. Shows Mest could do vulnerability without losing their identity.
