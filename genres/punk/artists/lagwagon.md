# Lagwagon — Deep Dive

## Overview

Lagwagon is an American punk rock band from Goleta, California (adjacent to Santa Barbara), formed in 1990 out of the ashes of a local speed-metal outfit called Section 8. The band has consisted of Joey Cape (vocals, guitar), Chris Flippin (guitar), and a rotating cast of bassists and drummers across nine studio albums, with the current lineup stabilized since 2010: Cape, Flippin, Chris Rest (guitar), Joe Raposo (bass), and Dave Raun (drums).

Lagwagon was the first band ever signed to Fat Wreck Chords — the independent punk label founded by NOFX's Fat Mike and Erin Burkett in 1990 — and their debut *Duh* was the label's first full-length release. That positioning wasn't just historical trivia; as the first signing, Lagwagon directly informed the musical direction Fat Wreck would pursue, helping define the label's identity: high-energy punk with pop melodic sophistication and technical musicianship.

The band is widely cited as one of the most significant acts in 90s skate punk. Critics have described them as "besting NOFX at their own game — faster and more technical, with better lyrics and melodies." While they never pursued mainstream success and turned down major label offers during the mid-90s punk explosion, they built a devoted international following through relentless touring and consistently strong records. Joey Cape's introspective, emotionally vulnerable songwriting set Lagwagon apart from the political satire (NOFX) and straightforward aggression (most skate punk) of their peers — a quality that only deepened through devastating personal losses.

The band's history is marked by two tragedies that profoundly shaped their music: the suicide of founding drummer Derrick Plourde in 2005, and the death of close friend and collaborator Tony Sly (No Use for a Name) in 2012.

## Members

### Joey Cape — Vocals, Guitar (1990–present)

- **Full name:** Randall Joseph Cape
- **Born:** November 16, 1966, Santa Barbara, California
- **Background:** Father is a physicist. Has two siblings. Has a daughter named Violet (wrote the song "Violet" for her). Santa Barbara native — connected to the local punk/skate scene from the start.
- **Early connection to RKL:** Helped form Rich Kids on LSD in the early 1980s by recruiting vocalist Jason Sears. Was initially slated as second guitarist before stepping away.
- **Vocal style:** Transitions seamlessly between "a soothing crooner and a ferocious anger-drenched vocalist." Deceptively casual delivery — often described as "lazily sung melodic vocals" — over technically complex instrumentation. His range and vocal diversity set Lagwagon apart from peers whose singers lacked variation.
- **Songwriting:** Writes virtually all Lagwagon material. Usually hears a melody first, then puts music behind it, brings it to the band. Does his best writing in solitude; long drives alone are his preferred atmosphere for lyrics. Influences: The Ramones, Elvis Costello.
- **What drives him:** "Life. Or more specifically life experience and the pain, beauty and knowledge that comes with it. Truth is stranger and more potent than fiction." But notes the bittersweet reality that age reduces surprise: "losing your muse one day at a time."
- **Side projects:** Bad Astronaut, Me First and the Gimme Gimmes (guitar, 1995–2025), Scorpios (with Tony Sly), extensive solo career (see dedicated section below).
- **Labels founded:** My Records (1996, released Nerf Herder's debut; defunct), One Week Records (current — artists record albums in 7 days at his home studio).
- **Production credits:** The Ataris, Nerf Herder, Ridel High.

### Chris Flippin — Guitar (1990–present)

- **Born:** ~1968
- **Founding member** — one of only two members (with Cape) present since inception.
- **Background:** Part of the original Section 8 speed-metal lineup before the transition to punk. Based in Santa Barbara.
- **Equipment:** Gibson Les Paul guitars, Marshall amplifiers, Seymour Duncan pickups (Vapor Trail, 805, Pearly Gates, '59 Model).
- **Role:** Core guitar continuity from the band's early skate punk roots. Dual-guitar attack with Rest (or Dewey in early years) emphasizes melody support rather than distraction — guitar lines continuously work to balance the song.

### Chris Rest — Guitar (1997–present)

- **Born:** ~1967, San Gabriel, California; raised in Ventura, Oxnard, and Goleta
- **Founding member of Rich Kids on LSD (RKL)** when they formed in 1982 — RKL's only constant member across their entire career. Originally recruited Joey Cape as a potential second guitarist for RKL.
- **Joined Lagwagon in 1997**, replacing Ken Stringfellow. Has appeared on all Lagwagon releases from *Let's Talk About Feelings* onward.
- **Also played in:** No Use for a Name (from August 2009), The Other (with RKL's Bomer Manzullo, 1996–97), Mad Caddies (guest on *Rock the Plank*, 2001), King City (SF-based fusion group with Joe Raposo).
- **Influences:** Bad Brains, Youth Brigade, Kiss, AC/DC.
- **Currently active** with the 2024 RKL reunion (with Barry Ward, Joe Raposo, Dave Raun).

### Joe Raposo — Bass (2010–present)

- **Born:** 1970, of Portuguese descent
- **Day job:** QA engineer lead at Zynga
- **Joined RKL as bassist in 1987** at age 17. Maximumrocknroll called him "flat out the best bass player in the state of California" in 1995.
- **Also played for:** The Real McKenzies, Mad Caddies, the Dwarves (live fill-in), King City (with Chris Rest).
- **Replaced Jesse Buglione** in Lagwagon in 2010. Plays on *Hang* and *Railer*.
- **Grew up in San Mateo** on the same block as Dave Raun.

- **Born:** June 7, 1970, Palo Alto, California. Raised in San Mateo. Lives in Fresno.
- **Began playing drums in 1981** in middle school concert and marching bands.
- **Joined RKL in 1992**, drummed until their 1996 hiatus (contributed to *Riches to Rags*, 1994). Reunited with RKL in 2024.

### Former Members

#### Derrick Plourde — Drums (1990–1995, deceased)

- **Born:** October 17, 1971, Goleta, California
- **Died:** March 30, 2005 (suicide, age 33)

- **Struggled with** drug addiction and suspected bipolar disorder (never formally diagnosed) throughout his adult life.

## The Derrick Plourde Story

Derrick William Plourde (October 17, 1971 — March 30, 2005) was Lagwagon's founding drummer and one of the most technically gifted drummers in punk history. His story is central to understanding the band's emotional trajectory.

**The talent:** Plourde's work on the first three Lagwagon albums — especially *Hoss* — set a standard for skate punk drumming that has never been surpassed. His speed, precision, and musicality went beyond what the genre demanded. His intricate double-stroke rolls impressed drum teachers, and his influence extended beyond punk — Travis Barker sought him out backstage as a young drummer to ask questions.

**The departure:** He left Lagwagon in 1995 after the *Hoss* world tour. Sources vary on whether he departed voluntarily or was let go, but the underlying issue was drug addiction that the band could no longer work around.

**Post-Lagwagon:** He stayed active — The Ataris (1997, debut album *Anywhere but Here*), Mad Caddies, RKL, Jaws, and most significantly Bad Astronaut (2001–2005), which reunited him with Joey Cape in a studio project that channeled their pop and alt-rock influences.

**The death:** Plourde died by suicide on March 30, 2005, at age 33. He had battled drug addiction and suspected bipolar disorder throughout his adult life. Joey Cape said he and others close to Plourde had felt "it's not if, it's when."

**The aftermath:**
- Lagwagon's *Resolve* (2005) was written entirely in response to Plourde's death, recorded just seven months after. The album is described as "hellbent on catharsis, much harder than anything since *Hoss*" — channeling grief into some of the band's most powerful material. The music video for "Heartbreaking Music" consisted almost entirely of photographs of Plourde.
- Bad Astronaut's *Twelve Small Steps, One Giant Disappointment* (2006) featured some of Plourde's final recordings. Cape then dissolved the band: "without Derrick, there is no Bad Astronaut."
- NOFX's "Doornails" (first verse, from *Wolves in Wolves' Clothing*) is about Plourde.

## Albums

### Duh (1992)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords (the label's first full-length release)
- **Recorded:** January 1992 at Westbeach Recorders. **Recorded and mixed in 4 days.**
- **Producer:** Fat Mike
- **Released:** October 1992
- **13 tracks:** "Tragic Vision," "Foiled Again," "Bury the Hatchet," "Angry Days," "Noble End," "Child Inside," "Bad Moon Rising," "Beer Goggles," "Parents Guide to Living," "Mr. Coffee," "Of Mind and Matter," "Stop Whining," "Lag Wagon"
- **Sound:** Primarily minor-key, angry, raw. Speed-metal roots audible. Became one of Fat Wreck's highest-selling releases despite never charting.
- **2011 Reissue:** Expanded with 22 previously unreleased Section 8 demos (1989 and 1991), revealing the band's thrash-y origins.

### Trashed (1994)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Recorded:** 1993 at Westbeach Recorders
- **Producer:** Fat Mike
- **Released:** January 4, 1994
- **Sound:** Major step forward — added pop hooks and killer melodies while staying thrashy. AllMusic: **4.5/5 stars**. *Punk Planet* called it "vital."
- **Key tracks:** "Island of Shame" (mature lyrics about small-town homophobia), "Know It All" (confronting sellout accusations), "Brown Eyed Girl" (double-time Van Morrison cover — "set the template for Me First and the Gimme Gimmes"), "Goin' South," "Give It Back," "Rust," "Whipping Boy," "Bye For Now"
- **Music video** for "Island of Shame"
- **Significance:** Established the formula — thrashy skate punk with emotional and melodic depth.

### Hoss (1995)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Released:** November 21, 1995
- **Producer:** Ryan Greene
- **Last album with Derrick Plourde and Shawn Dewey**
- **All songs written by Joey Cape**
- **Widely considered Lagwagon's magnum opus** and a defining skate punk album. Punknews: "where Lagwagon locked in their sound and achieved skatepunk perfection." Album of the Year users: "Lagwagon's magnum opus."
- **Derrick Plourde's drumming magnum opus** — "took skatepunk drumming to places it has never been before."
- **Key tracks:** "Violins" (probably their most well-known song), "Razor Burn" (lighthearted breakup song, popular video), "Ride the Snake" (friend's heroin struggle, Flippin on wah pedal), "Move the Car" (odd time signatures, technical showcase), "Weak," "Black Eyes," "Bombs Away," "Rifle" (intro: "one of the greatest documents of drumming prowess in punk history")
- **Followed by world tour** across US, Europe, Australia, Japan, with multiple dates alongside NOFX.
- **Currently being performed live in full** on a 2026 Australian tour celebrating the album's 30th anniversary.

### Double Plaidinum (1997)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Released:** August 12, 1997
- **Producers:** Joey Cape and Ryan Greene at Motor Studios
- **First album without original lineup** — Dave Raun (drums), Ken Stringfellow (guitar, temporary)
- **Billboard Heatseekers: #20**
- **Sound:** "The biggest outlier of Lagwagon music" — slowed tempo, not easily categorized as skate punk. Mixed reception: some felt it lacked energy; others argue the thirteen tracks "are arguably the band's best" due to consistency.
- **Key tracks:** "Alien 8," "Making Friends," "Failure," "Choke," "To All My Friends," "Confession," "Bad Scene"
- **Notable:** Stringfellow's diverse contributions (brass, woodwinds, acoustic guitar). Includes covers of Scorpions and DEVO.

### Let's Talk About Feelings (1998)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Chris Rest on board** as permanent second guitarist (replacing Stringfellow)
- **Billboard Heatseekers** (position unconfirmed)
- **Sound:** "Probably the poppiest of their albums" — moved toward an "indie punk vibe." Works exceptionally well as a cohesive whole; described as feeling like "one long track. In a good way."
- **Key track:** **"May 16"** — the band's most widely known song, later featured in *Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 2*.
- **Significance:** After this record, Cape channeled his pop/alt-rock leanings into Bad Astronaut; Lagwagon would never revisit this territory.

### Let's Talk About Leftovers (2000) — Compilation

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- Collection of B-sides, compilation tracks, and demos. Released during the band's hiatus when all members were working on side projects.

### Blaze (2003)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **First studio album in five years**
- **Billboard 200: #172** (first time on the Billboard 200)
- **14 tracks, 41 minutes** (notably long; *Let's Talk About Feelings* was only 25 minutes)
- **Sound:** Shows Bad Astronaut influence — first track "Burn" has laid-back tempo and mellow arpeggiated chords. "More straightforward punk record."
- **Key tracks:** "Burn," "E Dagger," "Dancing the Collapse," "I Must be Hateful," "Falling Apart," "Lullaby," "Billionaire," "Tomorrow is Heartbreak"
- **Punknews:** "Worth the wait, but not quite up to par with *Trashed* for diversity and not quite up to *Hoss* in terms of overall quality."

### Resolve (2005)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Released:** November 1, 2005
- **Dedicated to Derrick Plourde** (died March 30, 2005)
- **All songs written shortly after Plourde's death** — the album came only 7 months after
- **Last album with bassist Jesse Buglione**
- **Sound:** "Dark riffage," "hellbent on catharsis, much harder than anything since *Hoss*." Punknews: "they did something far better than anything I have heard from them since *Hoss*" — "something so emotional, yet so full of energy that it captures the very essence of what Lagwagon is all about."
- **Key track:** "Heartbreaking Music" — music video consisted almost entirely of photographs of Plourde
- **Hidden track:** "The Chemist" (Track 12.3, unlisted)
- **Significance:** Grief transformed into arguably the band's most emotionally powerful album. Did not sound rushed despite rapid creation.

### Hang (2014)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Released:** October 28, 2014
- **Producers:** Bill Stevenson and Jason Livermore at The Blasting Room, Fort Collins, CO
- **First album in 9 years**; first with bassist Joe Raposo
- **Billboard 200: #95** — the band's highest-ever chart position
- **Sound:** Continued *Resolve*'s direction — "dark riffage, murky but crisp production, and Joey Cape sounding angrier than ever before."

### Railer (2019)

- **Label:** Fat Wreck Chords
- **Released:** October 4, 2019
- **Producer:** Cameron Webb (Alkaline Trio, Motorhead)
- **12 songs, 35 minutes** — no filler
- **Sound:** Joey Cape's stated intent: "I really specifically tried to make it feel and sound like an old Lagwagon song, so I borrowed a lot from early records for it. The rules have to be that RAILER feels like something Lagwagon might have done in the old days."
- **Closes with** a punk cover of Journey's "Faithfully"
- **Critical reception overwhelmingly positive:**
  - Rockfreaks: "some of the all-time best material Lagwagon has written is on this album, no exaggeration" — "a genre masterpiece and a new benchmark"
  - Distorted Sound: "both [Green Day and Blink-182] wish they sounded like Lagwagon in 2019"
  - Punknews: "fresh, invigorating thrashers" that tapped early sound while retaining growth

## Musical Analysis — What Makes Lagwagon Sound Like Lagwagon

### Vocals

Joey Cape's voice is the defining instrument. He transitions seamlessly between crooning intimacy and ferocious anger within a single song. His delivery is deceptively casual — "lazily sung melodic vocals" riding over technically demanding instrumentation. Where most skate punk vocalists operate in one register (shouted), Cape operates across a full emotional and dynamic range. This vocal flexibility is what allows Lagwagon to be simultaneously one of the fastest bands in their genre and one of the most emotionally nuanced.

### Guitar

Dual-guitar attack (Flippin + Rest/Dewey) built on Gibson Les Pauls through Marshall amps with Seymour Duncan pickups. The guitars emphasize melody support — constantly working to balance the song rather than showboating. Crisp, powerful tone. The band's speed-metal roots (Section 8) give their riffs a heavier edge and more sophisticated harmonic vocabulary than many punk peers.

### Drums

A defining element across both eras. Derrick Plourde (1990–1995) was legendary — his work on *Hoss* set a standard for skate punk drumming that has never been surpassed. Speed, precision, and musicality beyond what the genre demanded. Dave Raun (1996–present) brought his own high-speed precision, influenced by Buddy Rich, John Bonham, and Bill Stevenson (Descendents), maintaining the band's rhythmic drive through the lineup change.

### Tempo and Structure

Generally rapid tempos (180–200+ BPM, characteristic of skate punk), but not one-dimensional. Songs like "Move the Car" use odd time signatures that "shouldn't work, but somehow do." Start-stop rhythms, tempo shifts, and structural surprises keep the speed from becoming monotonous. The metal roots enable heavier, more technically varied passages than most punk bands attempt.

### Melodic Complexity

More melodically sophisticated than most skate punk peers. The hooks are real hooks — not just fast power chords with a shout on top. Cape's melodies are distinctive and memorable, carried by his vocal range and the dual-guitar interplay. This melodic richness is what critics mean when they say Lagwagon was "faster and more technical, with better lyrics and melodies" than NOFX.

| Album | Year | Sound |
|-------|------|-------|
| Duh | 1992 | Minor-key, angry, raw — speed-metal roots audible |
| Trashed | 1994 | Added pop hooks, killer melodies, still thrashy |

## Lyrical Themes

Joey Cape's songwriting stands distinctly apart from most punk peers. While NOFX leans political and humorous, and many skate punk bands favor straightforward themes, Cape is deeply introspective and philosophical.

### Core Themes

- **Depression and emotional pain:** Lyrics frequently explore vulnerability, hurt, the experience of feeling defeated. His pandemic album *A Good Year to Forget* exemplifies this — "a mind-dump of some of the most sincere songs he has ever created."
- **Relationships and self-doubt:** Personal struggle, breakups, the difficulty of human connection.
- **Loss and grief:** Especially prominent on *Resolve* (Plourde's death) and work related to Tony Sly's passing.
- **Philosophical resilience:** Cape's view: "there are many times in your life when you feel defeated and the only recourse is just letting go" — sometimes problems are "passing things you just need to move on from or get through to learn from."
- **Social observation:** Not overtly political like Bad Religion or Propagandhi, but songs like "Island of Shame" (homophobia/small-town close-mindedness) and "Know It All" (scene politics) show sharp awareness.
- **Humor alongside darkness:** Lagwagon "specialize in high-octane, witty gems that couch surprisingly emotional lyrics in heavy guitars."

### Contrast with Peers

Where NOFX offers wit and political satire, and Strung Out offers metallic aggression, Lagwagon occupies a uniquely emotional space in the Fat Wreck catalog — "Joey Cape's trademark bleeding heart lyrics." This emotional vulnerability, delivered at skate punk velocity, is what makes Lagwagon irreplaceable.

## Connection to Fat Wreck Chords

- **First band signed** to the label when Fat Mike and Erin Burkett established it in 1990.
- *Duh* was the label's **first full-length release**.
- Joey Cape and Fat Mike have a close personal friendship. Cape: "one of my best buddies, and I hang around with him a lot."
- Cape moved to San Francisco and worked at Fat Wreck Chords (as did Chris Shiflett, whom Cape helped get an office job there).
- Fat Mike produced *Duh* and *Trashed*.
- The band has remained on Fat Wreck for their **entire career** — over 30 years with no label changes.
- Extensive touring partnerships with NOFX and other Fat Wreck bands.
- Participated in the **Fat Wrecked for 25 Years** tour.
- Among the most important Fat Wreck bands alongside NOFX, Strung Out, Good Riddance, Propagandhi, No Use for a Name, and Face to Face.

## Joey Cape's Solo Work

### Solo Albums

- **Bridge** (2008) — Debut. Stripped-down versions of Lagwagon songs plus new originals.
- **Doesn't Play Well with Others** (2011) — Songs released individually starting 2010.
- **Stitch Puppy** (2015)
- **One Week Record** (2016) — On his own One Week Records label.
- **Let Me Know When You Give Up** (2019) — "A bit more philosophical," inspired partly by leaving social media.
- **A Good Year to Forget** (2021) — Recorded in COVID isolation. "Sullen, somber, introspective tunes."

### Bad Astronaut

Formed during Lagwagon's hiatus as an experimental indie rock/alt-rock studio project. Members: Joey Cape (vocals/guitar), Marko DeSantis (bass), Angus Cooke (cello/vocals), Thom Flowers (guitar), Todd Capps (keys/vocals), Derrick Plourde (drums on first two albums).

- **Acrophobe** (2001) — Debut, Honest Don's Records
- **Houston: We Have a Drinking Problem** (2002) — Expanded sound with cello, keyboards
- **Twelve Small Steps, One Giant Disappointment** (2006) — Final album, Fat Wreck Chords. Features some of Plourde's final recordings.
- **Universe** box set (2021) — Limited edition (1,555 copies) colored vinyl reissue.
- Dissolved after Plourde's death. Cape: "without Derrick, there is no Bad Astronaut."

### Me First and the Gimme Gimmes

Punk rock cover band/supergroup: Fat Mike (NOFX), Spike Slawson (Swingin' Utters), Chris Shiflett (Foo Fighters), Joey Cape (guitar/backing vocals), Dave Raun (drums, until 2019). Cape joined in 1995. Rebranded as Spike and the Gimme Gimmes in 2025; Cape stepped back from touring.

Lagwagon's double-time cover of "Brown Eyed Girl" on *Trashed* (1994) is credited with setting the template for Me First and the Gimme Gimmes' entire approach.

### One Week Records

Cape's label. Artists record 10 songs in 7 days (or 5 songs in 3 days for a "One Weekend Record") at his home studio. Philosophy: "I don't want to hear what the studio sounds like, I want to hear what the artist sounds like." Artists include Chris Cresswell, Zach Quinn (PEARS), Yotam Ben Horin, and others.

## The Tony Sly Connection

Anthony James Sly (November 4, 1970 — July 31, 2012) was the lead singer/guitarist of No Use for a Name, another cornerstone Fat Wreck Chords band.

**Relationship with Cape:** One of the great alliances in melodic punk. Both shared a vision of the song as a space for vulnerability. They toured together extensively — "wherever there was a dim barroom with a microphone, you could find Joey and Tony with their acoustic guitars."

**Collaborations:**
- *Acoustic* (2004) — Split acoustic album, Fat Wreck Chords. Acoustic renditions of Lagwagon and No Use for a Name songs plus two new exclusive tracks.
- *Acoustic Volume Two* (2012) — Completed just days before Sly's death.
- **Scorpios** (2011) — Band with Cape, Sly, Jon Snodgrass, Brian Wahlstrom.

**Sly's death:** Died in his sleep on July 31, 2012, at age 41. His last performance was July 29, 2012, in Gainesville, Florida, with Joey Cape. They closed with a cover of NOFX's "Linoleum."

**Aftermath:** No Use for a Name disbanded. Fat Wreck released *The Songs of Tony Sly: A Tribute* (November 2013). NOFX wrote "I'm So Sorry Tony." Lagwagon contributed "One More Song." Another devastating loss for Cape after Plourde — further deepening the emotional weight in his songwriting.

## Legacy and Influence

**Defining role:** "When it comes to the 90s skate punk genre, few bands hold a more prestigious position in establishing the sound of the era than Lagwagon."

**Who they influenced:** Cited as a direct influence on Blink-182, Good Charlotte, and New Found Glory. Newer bands credit Lagwagon's breakneck pacing, album cohesion, and melodic depth.

**Comparison with peers:**
- **vs. NOFX:** "Faster and more technical, with better lyrics and melodies." NOFX is more political/humorous; Lagwagon more introspective/emotional. Both are Fat Wreck pillars.
- **vs. Strung Out:** Strung Out fuses punk with metal for a heavier, more metallic edge. Lagwagon is more melodic and introspective.
- **vs. No Use for a Name:** Close peers and friends (especially through Tony Sly). Both occupy melodic/emotional territory but with different vocal styles.
- **vs. Propagandhi:** Propagandhi is more overtly political and technically progressive. Lagwagon balances technical skill with emotional accessibility.
- **vs. Bad Religion:** Bad Religion pioneered the sound Lagwagon evolved. Lagwagon is less academic/philosophical but more personally vulnerable.

**The Fat Wreck ecosystem:** As the first signing, Lagwagon directly shaped the label's musical identity. The "Fat Wreck sound" — high-energy fusion of punk aggression, pop melodies, and technical guitar interplay — is essentially Lagwagon's sound codified into a label aesthetic.

**Cultural footprint:** "May 16" in *Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 2* introduced the band to a generation of skaters and gamers who might never have found Fat Wreck Chords otherwise.

## Reference Tracks (Lagwagon Essential Listening)

### Defining the Sound
- **"Violins"** (Hoss, 1995) — Probably their most well-known song. Peak Lagwagon — fast, melodic, emotionally devastating.
- **"May 16"** (Let's Talk About Feelings, 1998) — Their most widely recognized track via Tony Hawk's Pro Skater 2. Poppy, hook-driven, accessible.
- **"Razor Burn"** (Hoss, 1995) — Lighthearted breakup song with popular music video. Shows the humor alongside the hurt.
- **"Island of Shame"** (Trashed, 1994) — Mature lyrics about small-town homophobia. Minor-key, emotionally sophisticated.

### Technical Showcases
- **"Rifle"** (Hoss, 1995) — Plourde's drumming apex. Intro described as "one of the greatest documents of drumming prowess in punk history."
- **"Move the Car"** (Hoss, 1995) — Odd time signatures that shouldn't work but do. Technical showcase.
- **"Ride the Snake"** (Hoss, 1995) — Friend's heroin struggle. Flippin's wah pedal work.

### Emotional Core
- **"Heartbreaking Music"** (Resolve, 2005) — Plourde tribute. Music video of his photographs.
- **"Know It All"** (Trashed, 1994) — Confronting sellout accusations. Cape at his most defiant.
- **"Angry Days"** (Duh, 1992) — Early raw anger, speed-metal roots showing.

### Evolution
- **"Alien 8"** (Double Plaidinum, 1997) — The outlier era. Slower, more textured.
- **"Burn"** (Blaze, 2003) — Bad Astronaut influence. Laid-back tempo, mellow arpeggiated chords.
- **"Faithfully"** (Railer, 2019) — Punk cover of Journey. Full circle — sincerity without irony.

### Cover
- **"Brown Eyed Girl"** (Trashed, 1994) — Double-time Van Morrison cover that "set the template for Me First and the Gimme Gimmes."
