# Latin

## Genre Overview

Latin music encompasses rhythmic traditions from Spanish, Portuguese, and African influences across the Americas. From the dance floors of Havana to the ballrooms of Buenos Aires, Latin music is defined by complex polyrhythms, infectious grooves, and emotional depth. Whether it's the infectious reggaeton beats of modern Puerto Rico, the sophisticated harmonies of bossa nova, or the passionate storytelling of bachata, Latin music spans centuries of cultural evolution while maintaining its signature fusion of rhythm and soul.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Percussion-driven (congas, timbales, bongos, claves), brass (trumpets, trombones), piano, guitar, bass
- **Rhythm**: Complex polyrhythms, syncopation, clave patterns
- **Vocals**: Passionate, expressive, often prominent with call-and-response
- **Production**: Ranges from organic acoustic to modern electronic
- **Structure**: Verse-chorus with instrumental breaks, percussion solos, rhythmic interplay

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Reggaeton | Modern urban, trap-influenced rhythm, electronic production | Daddy Yankee, Bad Bunny, J Balvin |
| Salsa | Cuban dance music, brassy, energetic, polyrhythmic | Tito Puente, Celia Cruz, Salsa Timba |
| Bachata | Dominican romantic ballad, guitar-driven, emotional vocals | Juan Luis Guerra, Aventura, Prince Royce |
| Cumbia | Colombian rhythmic dance, accordion, percussion, festive | Celso Piña, Willie Colón, La Sonora Dinamita |
| Bossa Nova | Brazilian sophisticated jazz-samba fusion, smooth, harmonic | João Gilberto, Antônio Carlos Jobim, Stan Getz |
| Latin Pop | Commercial, crossover appeal, modern production | Ricky Martin, Shakira, Marc Anthony |
| Norteño | Mexican accordion-based, storytelling, folk roots | Los Tigres del Norte, Intocable, Grupo Frontera |
| Mariachi | Mexican brass band, romantic, festive, traditional | Mariachi Vargas de Tecalitlán, El Coro de México |
