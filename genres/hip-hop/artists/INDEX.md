## Reference Tracks

### Brock Berrigan
- "Diamond in the Rough" (Diamond in the Rough, 2015) — Title track of his breakout album; warm jazz sample flip over boom-bap drums, samples Armando Manzanero
- "The Celebration Song" — One of his most popular tracks; upbeat, feel-good, jazzy, groovy, and irresistibly head-nodding
- "Four Walls and an Amplifier" — Fan favorite showcasing layered production: sampled textures building into a full, warm composition
- "Monument Valley" (The Narrows, 2018) — Named for the iconic desert landscape; spacious production evoking wide open desert spaces
- "Angel's Landing" (Point Pleasant, 2017) — Named for the Zion National Park trail; atmospheric, bridging the New York era and Utah influence
- "Canyonlands" (Cadillac Mountain, 2018) — At 3:01, one of his longer tracks; demonstrates how the desert landscape expanded his compositions
- "Cadillac Mountain" feat. Philanthrope (Cadillac Mountain, 2018) — Collaboration with fellow chillhop producer; Berrigan's sound meshing with the broader lo-fi community
- "Sometime" feat. Allem Iversom (The Scenic Route, 2019) — Rare vocal feature adding a different dimension to typically instrumental work
- "King of the Rats" (The Narrows, 2018) — Highlighted by Beautiful Song of the Week; standalone composition rather than background music
- "Lee's Ferry" (Paradise Canyon, 2024) — Mature composition showing a decade-plus of refined production without losing essential warmth
- "License to Chill" (The Narrows, 2018) — Peak laid-back Berrigan: smooth, easy, effortlessly cool
