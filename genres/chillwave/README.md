# CHILLWAVE

## Genre Overview

Chillwave is a lo-fi electronic pop genre that emerged in the late 2000s, characterized by hazy, washed-out synths, dreamy atmospheres, and nostalgic 80s-90s aesthetics. The genre emphasizes mood and texture over technical complexity, creating introspective soundscapes that feel intimate and emotionally resonant. Vocals are often treated with reverb and effects to blend seamlessly into the sonic landscape, and production is deliberately lo-fi or "imperfectly perfect." Chillwave evokes feelings of nostalgia, melancholy, and peaceful contemplation.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Analog synths, vintage drum machines, lo-fi percussion, occasionally sparse guitar or bass, minimal live instrumentation
- **Vocals**: Treated heavily with reverb/echo, washed-out or ethereal delivery, often secondary to the instrumental texture
- **Production**: Lo-fi or intentionally degraded sound quality, warm tape saturation, compressed dynamics, heavy use of effects and processing
- **Energy/Mood**: Relaxed, introspective, melancholic, nostalgic, dreamy, peaceful yet subtly emotional
- **Structure**: Minimalist song structures, long instrumental intros/outros, looping rhythmic elements, gradual builds rather than dramatic shifts

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Glo-Fi** | Glowing, uplifting variant with more bass and rhythmic drive; "glowing" synths with wider hooks | Neon Indian, Memory Cassette |
| **Hypnagogic Pop** | Trippy, oneiric take on pop songwriting; lyrics drift in and out of focus; more experimental structure | Ariel Pink, Oneohtrix Point Never |
| **Dream Pop Electronic** | Electronic take on dream pop; ethereal vocals layered heavily in reverb; lush synth arrangements | Purity Ring, Slowdive (electronic moments) |
| **Synthwave Chill** | Retro 80s synth vibes with more uptempo energy; neon-noir aesthetics with chilled delivery | Com Truise, The Midnight |
| **Chillgaze** | Blend of chillwave and shoegaze; distorted guitars under hazy synths; ethereal vocals | Tycho, Tame Impala (early) |
| **Vapor Chill** | Ambient, drift-focused variant; extremely minimalist; sample-based; influenced by vaporwave aesthetics | Blank Banshee, Yung Lean |
