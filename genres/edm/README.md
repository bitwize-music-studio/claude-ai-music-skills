# Electronic Dance Music (EDM)

## Genre Overview

Electronic Dance Music (EDM) is built on synthesizers, drum machines, and digital production, with an emphasis on rhythm, energy, and danceability. From the hypnotic pulses of techno to the euphoric builds of progressive house, EDM spans decades of club culture and festival excellence while maintaining its core focus on the beat and the dance floor.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Synthesizers, drum machines, sequencers, digital sound design
- **Vocals**: Often minimal or guest-featured, sometimes robotic or heavily processed
- **Production**: Studio-centric, heavily electronic, layered soundscapes
- **Energy**: High-energy, beat-driven, designed for dance and movement
- **Structure**: Builds, drops, breakdowns, extended intros/outros, 4/4 time signature

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| House | Four-on-the-floor, soulful, groovy, club-ready | Daft Punk, Disclosure, Calvin Harris |
| Techno | Minimal, hypnotic, industrial, repetitive | Richie Hawtin, Amelie Lens, Charlotte de Witte |
| Trance | Euphoric, uplifting, emotional builds | Armin van Buuren, Above & Beyond, Tiësto |
| Dubstep | Heavy bass, wobble, syncopated rhythms | Skrillex, Deadmau5, Excision |
| Drum and Bass | Fast breakbeats (170+ BPM), rolling basslines | Logistics, Calibre, Andy C |
| Hardstyle | Hard-hitting kicks, distorted leads, energetic | Headhunterz, Wildstylez, Showtek |
| Progressive House | Layered builds, hypnotic grooves, sophisticated | Carl Cox, Sasha & John Digweed, Maya Jane Coles |
