# Folk

## Genre Overview

Folk music emphasizes acoustic instrumentation, storytelling, and traditional or traditional-influenced songwriting. From bright coffee-shop singer-songwriter to dark murder ballads, folk prioritizes narrative, emotional authenticity, and organic sound.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Acoustic guitar, banjo, fiddle, mandolin, upright bass, piano
- **Vocals**: Clear, emotive, storytelling delivery - ranges from warm to haunting
- **Lyrics**: Narrative-driven, personal, literary, often rooted in tradition
- **Production**: Organic, acoustic-forward, minimal to moderately produced
- **Tone**: Intimate, authentic, ranges from cozy to devastating

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Dark Americana | Murder ballads, Southern Gothic, haunting | Gillian Welch, Nick Cave, 16 Horsepower |
| Singer-Songwriter | Personal, confessional, acoustic | Joni Mitchell, James Taylor, Elliott Smith |
| Traditional Folk | Rooted in folk traditions, ballads | Bob Dylan (early), Joan Baez, Woody Guthrie |
| Appalachian | Mountain music, old-time, bluegrass influence | Doc Watson, Ralph Stanley, Old Crow Medicine Show |
| Contemporary Folk | Modern production, indie sensibility | Iron & Wine, Fleet Foxes, Bon Iver |
