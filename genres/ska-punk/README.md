# Ska Punk

## Genre Overview

Ska punk blends the upbeat, offbeat rhythms of ska with the energy and attitude of punk rock. Characterized by brass sections, fast tempos, and high-energy performances, the genre emerged in the late 1980s and exploded in popularity during the 1990s third-wave ska movement.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Brass sections (trumpet, trombone, saxophone), guitar, bass, drums
- **Rhythm**: Offbeat guitar "skank," walking basslines, driving punk drums
- **Vocals**: Energetic, often shouted or gang vocals
- **Tempo**: Fast, high-energy (160-200+ BPM)
- **Tone**: Fun, chaotic, irreverent, sometimes political

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Third Wave Ska | 90s mainstream ska punk | Reel Big Fish, Less Than Jake, Goldfinger |
| Skacore | Heavier, more aggressive | Streetlight Manifesto, Catch 22 |
| Traditional Ska Punk | Closer to 2-Tone roots | The Specials, Operation Ivy |
