# Trip-Hop

## Genre Overview

Trip-hop is a downtempo electronic music genre that emerged in Bristol, UK during the early 1990s, blending hip-hop beats with atmospheric samples, psychedelic textures, and moody aesthetics. Characterized by slow, syncopated breakbeats (typically 85-110 BPM), layered instrumentation, and introspective lyrics, trip-hop creates a cinematic, introspective sound perfect for late-night listening. The genre draws from breakbeats, dub reggae, and ambient music, often incorporating organic instruments alongside electronic production. Trip-hop achieved mainstream recognition through acts like Massive Attack and Portishead, establishing a blueprint for downtempo electronic music that remains influential across multiple genres.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Layered synthesizers, sampled strings and horns, live guitars, bass lines, drum machines, turntables, organic percussion, field recordings, atmospheric pads
- **Vocals**: Breathy and intimate, often ethereal or melancholic, minimal presence, spoken word elements, blended low in the mix, alternatively dark and dramatic
- **Production**: Heavy use of reverb and delay, lo-fi and hifi aesthetics, heavy compression, analog warmth, layered sampling, cinematic arrangement, unconventional song structures
- **Energy/Mood**: Moody, introspective, dark, contemplative, hypnotic, cinematic, slightly unsettling, noir-influenced, late-night ambiance
- **Structure**: Typically 3-5 minute tracks, minimal chorus reliance, atmospheric build-ups, layered arrangements that evolve throughout, often features breakbeat-driven rhythm section beneath atmospheric layers

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Bristol Sound** | Original trip-hop blueprint from Bristol, UK; Massive Attack and Portishead established the template | Massive Attack, Portishead, Tricky |
| **Downtempo** | Broader category emphasizing relaxation and atmosphere; slow, ambient-leaning production | Thom Yorke, Amon Tobin, Bonobo |
| **Abstract Hip-Hop** | Trip-hop blended with experimental hip-hop; loose structure, producer-centric | DJ Shadow, Nightmares on Wax, Jlin |
| **Darkside/Dark Ambient Trip-Hop** | Heavier, more ominous take emphasizing dissonance and unease | Tricky, Godspeed You! Black Emperor, Burial |
| **Psychedelic Trip-Hop** | Trip-hop incorporating psych aesthetics; surreal samples, heavy reverb, vintage production | The Cinematic Orchestra, Porcupine Tree (earlier), Blonde Redhead |
| **Neo-Trip-Hop** | Contemporary takes on the genre; blending with modern production techniques and other genres | James Blake, Massive Attack (modern), Disclosure |
| **Dub Trip-Hop** | Heavy reggae/dub influence; spacious production, heavy bass, echo and reverb effects | Scientist, Lee Perry influences, Jah Wobble |
| **Jazz-Influenced Trip-Hop** | Incorporates jazz chord progressions, improvisation, and instrumentation | Ninja Tune roster, Kiasmos, Jaga Jazzist |
