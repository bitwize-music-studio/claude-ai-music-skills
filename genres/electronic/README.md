# Electronic

## Genre Overview

Electronic music encompasses a vast range of styles united by the use of electronic instruments, synthesizers, and digital production. From ambient soundscapes to driving dance beats, the genre prioritizes texture, atmosphere, and sonic experimentation.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Synthesizers, drum machines, samplers, digital production
- **Production**: Ranges from warm analog to crisp digital, often heavily layered
- **Vocals**: Optional - can be absent, processed, or central depending on subgenre
- **Mood**: Varies widely - meditative, energetic, dark, euphoric, introspective
- **Focus**: Sound design, atmosphere, rhythm, texture

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Ambient | Atmospheric, textural, meditative | Brian Eno, Aphex Twin (Selected Ambient Works) |
| Synthwave/Retrowave | 80s-inspired, neon aesthetics, cinematic | Kavinsky, Gunship, The Midnight |
| Synth-Pop | Melodic, accessible, 80s influence | Depeche Mode, Pet Shop Boys, CHVRCHES |
| IDM | Experimental, complex rhythms, cerebral | Aphex Twin, Autechre, Boards of Canada |
| New Age | Meditative, often acoustic-electronic blend | Enya, Vangelis, Kitaro |
| Chillout/Downtempo | Relaxed, atmospheric, smooth | Air, Massive Attack, Zero 7 |
