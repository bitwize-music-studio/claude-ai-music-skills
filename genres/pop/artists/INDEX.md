## Reference Tracks

### Ace of Base
- "All That She Wants" (Happy Nation, 1992) — The template; C#m, 94 BPM; modified one-drop reggae beat, Casio MIDI Horn riff, deep sub-bass, Linn Berggren's contralto lead; Juno-106 pads define the harmonic bed
- "The Sign" (Happy Nation / The Sign, 1993) — Korg Wavestation synth-whistle hook over busy panning percussion; biggest-selling single of 1994 in the US
- "Happy Nation" (Happy Nation, 1992) — Rare major-key outing; pulsing Juno pads, precise drum programming, sister harmonies at their most seamless
- "Beautiful Life" (The Bridge, 1995) — Max Martin's first US hit credit; 135 BPM, faster than signature range; the embryonic Cheiron Studios sound
- "Don't Turn Around" (Happy Nation, 1992) — Cover routed through Aswad's reggae version; reveals how deeply reggae influence permeated their approach

### Aqua
- "Barbie Girl" (Aquarium, 1997) — C#m, 130 BPM; THE bubblegum Eurodance track; vocal contrast as production technique; JV-2080 synth provides all non-vocal elements
- "Doctor Jones" (Aquarium, 1997) — Cm, 140 BPM; spacey pad under driving rhythm; hook density at its peak with independent hooks in every section
- "Turn Back Time" (Aquarium, 1997) — Ab major, 104 BPM; Lene drops character voice to reveal genuine mezzo-soprano; downtempo funk/soul
- "My Oh My" (Aquarium, 1997) — ~136 BPM; medieval bubblegum with harpsichord patches and sampled horse hooves
- "Cartoon Heroes" (Aquarius, 2000) — C# major, 128 BPM; most musically accomplished single; tighter production, anthemic chorus

### Carly Rae Jepsen
- "Run Away with Me" (E·MO·TION, 2015) — Soaring synth-pop with iconic saxophone intro; considered her masterpiece; emotional maximalism at its peak
- "Cut to the Feeling" (E·MO·TION Side B, 2017) — Pure pop euphoria; B-side that became a cultural touchstone and Pride anthem
- "Call Me Maybe" (Kiss, 2012) — Global mega-hit; expertly constructed pop hook; the song that started everything
- "All That" (E·MO·TION, 2015) — Dev Hynes collaboration; warmer R&B-inflected synth-pop; nocturnal texture
- "Want You in My Room" (Dedicated, 2019) — Jack Antonoff production; pulsing, propulsive, physically compelling disco-pop
- "Now That I Found You" (Dedicated, 2019) — Perfectly structured pop-disco; instantly catchy
- "The Loneliest Time" (The Loneliest Time, 2022) — Baroque duet with Rufus Wainwright; orchestral space-themed pop
- "Boy Problems" (E·MO·TION, 2015) — Self-aware synth-funk pop; humor and self-deprecation over driving groove

### La Bouche
- "Be My Lover" (Sweet Dreams, 1996) — C#m, 135 BPM; signature track; Thornton's vocal spans G#3-F#5; punchy synth riff is the defining instrumental hook
- "Sweet Dreams" (Sweet Dreams, 1996) — Bm, 134 BPM; debut single; laid the entire La Bouche template; i-VI-VII harmony over driving bass
- "Fallin' in Love" (Sweet Dreams, 1996) — ~138 BPM; cover of Hamilton Joe Frank & Reynolds; reveals how gospel training transforms standard material
- "I Love to Love" (Sweet Dreams, 1996) — Cm, ~138 BPM; Thornton at peak energy; vocal power without sacrificing precision
- "You Won't Forget Me" (A Moment of Love, 1997) — Later era; demonstrates formula durability with subtle production evolution

### Taylor Swift
- "Love Story" (Fearless, 2008) — Quintessential country crossover; narrative songwriting with anthemic chorus
- "You Belong with Me" (Fearless, 2008) — Perfect pop-country hook; the mainstream breakthrough
- "Blank Space" (1989, 2014) — Self-aware synth-pop masterpiece; full pop transformation
- "Style" (1989, 2014) — Atmospheric 80s-influenced synth-pop; sonically sophisticated
- "All Too Well (10 Minute Version)" (Red (Taylor's Version), 2021) — Long-form narrative storytelling; emotional bridge writing
- "Cardigan" (Folklore, 2020) — Piano-driven indie folk; the genre pivot
- "Exile" feat. Bon Iver (Folklore, 2020) — Chamber folk duet with dynamic build
- "Anti-Hero" (Midnights, 2022) — Confessional dream-pop; biggest streaming hit
