# Pop

## Genre Overview

Pop music prioritizes catchy melodies, accessible hooks, and broad commercial appeal. Built on strong vocal performances, polished production, and memorable choruses, pop spans from intimate acoustic ballads to high-energy dance tracks. The genre evolves continuously, absorbing influences from electronic, R&B, rock, and world music while maintaining focus on melody and emotional connection.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Varied (synths, drums, guitars, keyboards), production-focused
- **Vocals**: Prominent, melodic, often auto-tuned or processed
- **Production**: Polished, layered, carefully crafted arrangements
- **Energy**: Wide range from intimate to anthemic
- **Structure**: Strong hooks, repetitive choruses, often shorter song lengths

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Synth-Pop | Electronic, 80s-influenced, synthesizers | Depeche Mode, Pet Shop Boys, Chvrches |
| Dance-Pop | Upbeat, electronic production, radio-friendly | Dua Lipa, Britney Spears, Lady Gaga |
| Indie Pop | DIY sensibility, quirky melodies, bedroom pop | Vampire Weekend, Snail Mail, Mac DeMarco |
| Power Pop | Energetic, guitar-driven pop hooks | Cheap Trick, The Cars, Weezer |
| Pop Ballad | Emotional vocals, minimal arrangement, storytelling | Adele, Ed Sheeran, John Legend |
| Teen Pop | Youthful energy, aspirational lyrics, high production | Justin Timberlake, Ariana Grande, Shawn Mendes |
