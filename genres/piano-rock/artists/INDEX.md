## Reference Tracks

### Ben Folds Five
- "Jackson Cannery" (1995) — Genre-defining opener; aggressive piano riff declares independence
- "One Angry Dwarf and 200 Solemn Faces" (1997) — Revenge fantasy; triumphant key change
- "Brick" (1997) — Masterclass in restraint; quiet vocal, true story, #19 on Hot 100
- "Song for the Dumped" (1997) — Pure breakup fury in 3:19; hammered piano, distorted bass
- "Evaporated" (1997) — Six minutes of building emotional architecture; devastating coda
- "Army" (1999) — Gentle piano ballad building to full marching band arrangement
- "Narcolepsy" (1999) — String-laden, atmospheric, unsettling
- "Don't Change Your Plans" (1999) — Tender, heartbreaking ballad

### Ben Folds (Solo)
- "Rockin' the Suburbs" (2001) — Satirizes suburban malaise; bridges piano rock and pop
- "Still Fighting It" (2001) — Father-to-son tenderness; "Everybody knows it hurts to grow up"
- "The Luckiest" (2001) — Modern love song; no irony, vulnerability only
- "Fred Jones Part 2" (2001) — Novelistic empathy; piano and strings paint a layoff
- "Not the Same" (2001) — Builds to massive choral finale
- "Landed" (2005) — Soaring ballad about relief; melodically irresistible
- "Gracie" (2005) — Written for daughter; tender, specific, real
- "Late" (2005) — Elliott Smith elegy; quiet, devastating
- "You Don't Know Me" feat. Regina Spektor (2008) — Duet with spontaneous chemistry
- "Capable of Anything" (2015) — yMusic chamber arrangement; flips meaning darkly
- "Phone in a Pool" (2015) — yMusic pop-rock; anxious communication breakdown

### Elton John
- "Your Song" (1970) — The template; piano, voice, sincerity; one of the most perfect pop songs ever written
- "Tiny Dancer" (1971) — Orchestral piano rock perfection; sweeping strings, building emotional arc
- "Rocket Man" (1972) — Dreamy, melancholic; spaceflight as isolation metaphor; iconic piano riff
- "Bennie and the Jets" (1973) — Glam rock through the piano; rhythmic, funky piano riff IS the hook
- "Saturday Night's Alright for Fighting" (1973) — Piano rock as hard rock; aggressive, arena-filling
- "Someone Saved My Life Tonight" (1975) — Epic autobiographical ballad; churning piano octaves drive six minutes of tension
- "Don't Let the Sun Go Down on Me" (1974) — Orchestral power ballad template; quiet piano to full climax
- "I'm Still Standing" (1983) — Comeback anthem; defiant, joyful, uptempo piano pop-rock

### Billy Joel
- "Piano Man" (1973) — The genre's anthem; waltz-time piano, narrative storytelling, singalong chorus
- "Scenes from an Italian Restaurant" (1977) — Multi-movement piano rock epic; jazz opening to driving rock narrative
- "Movin' Out (Anthony's Song)" (1977) — Blue-collar frustration; driving piano riff with saxophone
- "Vienna" (1977) — Waltz-time meditation; tender piano building to full arrangement
- "It's Still Rock and Roll to Me" (1980) — Hard-edged new wave pivot; aggressive piano; first #1 single
- "Allentown" (1982) — Rust Belt despair through Beatles-esque production; social commentary
- "Uptown Girl" (1983) — Four Seasons-style doo-wop pop; genre mastery demonstration
- "The River of Dreams" (1993) — Gospel-influenced pop; handclaps and a cappella; final major hit
