# Afrobeats

## Genre Overview

Afrobeats is a vibrant, rhythm-driven genre rooted in West African musical traditions, combining traditional percussion with modern hip-hop, R&B, dancehall, and electronic influences. Born primarily in Nigeria, Afrobeats has evolved into a global phenomenon, characterized by infectious grooves, polyrhythmic patterns, and celebration-oriented energy that blends heritage with contemporary production.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Drums, percussion, synthesizers, guitars, bass, often layered with traditional African rhythms
- **Vocals**: Melodic, conversational, often featuring call-and-response, singing and rapping intermixed
- **Production**: Modern, polished, dance-oriented with heavy basslines and layered rhythms
- **Energy**: Uplifting, groove-based, dancefloor-ready with infectious rhythms
- **Structure**: Built on repetitive melodic hooks, layered percussion, and call-and-response elements

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Nigerian Afrobeats | Core Lagos sound, modern production, global appeal | Burna Boy, Wizkid, Davido, Olamide |
| Afropop | Pop-oriented, radio-friendly, melodic focus | Tiwa Savage, CKay, Rema, Tems |
| Afro-Fusion | Blends afrobeats with reggae, dancehall, amapiano | Afrobeats collective, Jada Kingdom |
| Amapiano | Piano-driven, house-influenced, South African | Sun-EL Musician, De Mthuda, DBN Gogo |
| Highlife | Traditional West African, uplifting, horns-driven | Highlife legends, modern interpreters |
