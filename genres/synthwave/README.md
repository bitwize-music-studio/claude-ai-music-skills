# Synthwave

## Genre Overview

Synthwave is a retro-futuristic electronic music genre that draws inspiration from 1980s film soundtracks, arcade video games, and synth-pop music. Characterized by lush synthesizer textures, analog warmth, and nostalgic aesthetics, synthwave evokes neon-lit cityscapes and digital dreamscapes. The genre emerged in the 2000s as a revival of 80s sound design principles, blending nostalgia with contemporary production techniques to create immersive, atmospheric soundscapes that feel both retro and timeless.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Analog and digital synthesizers, drum machines, vintage keyboards, bass synths, occasional guitars and orchestral strings
- **Vocals**: Often processed and atmospheric, ranging from robotic to human, frequently layered with effects; many tracks are instrumental
- **Production**: Heavy use of reverb, delay, and analog warmth; polished but organic sound; emphasis on texture over clarity
- **Energy/Mood**: Nostalgic yet futuristic, cinematic, dreamlike, introspective, sometimes dark or melancholic; evokes 1980s film noir and cyberpunk aesthetics
- **Structure**: Typically longer track lengths (4-6+ minutes); gradual builds, atmospheric intros, synth-led arrangements with emphasis on hooks and memorable melodies

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Outrun** | High-energy synthwave with driving rhythm, inspired by 80s action films and Miami Vice; focuses on momentum and propulsive beats | Kavinsky, Carpenter Brut, Perturbator |
| **Darksynth** | Darker, moodier synthwave with industrial influences; aggressive synths, minor keys, and dystopian atmospheres | Perturbator, Carpenter Brut, Gost |
| **Dreamwave** | Ethereal, atmospheric synthwave with lush pads; focuses on mood and emotion over rhythm; often minimal and meditative | Com Truise, Betamaxx, Mitch Murder |
| **Retrowave** | Directly emulates 80s pop and film score aesthetics; brighter, more melodic; emphasis on nostalgia and pastiche | Gunship, Perturbator, Lazerhawk |
| **Cyberpunk** | Futuristic, often darker synthwave with industrial and sci-fi influences; explores themes of dystopia and technology | Carpenter Brut, Perturbator, Gost |
| **Chillwave/Vaporwave** | Slower, ambient-leaning synthwave; incorporates lo-fi and vaporwave elements; smooth, relaxing soundscapes | Betamaxx, Lazerhawk (mellower tracks) |

## Reference Tracks

- Kavinsky - "Nightcall" - Iconic outrun anthem with driving bassline and nostalgic synth hooks
- Carpenter Brut - "Turbo Killer" - High-energy darksynth with aggressive synth stabs and industrial drums
- Perturbator - "Dangerous Days" - Darksynth masterpiece; brooding, ominous, cinematic atmosphere
- Gost - "Metal and Dust" - Industrial-leaning darksynth with dystopian aesthetic
- Lazerhawk - "Overdrive" - Melodic outrun with crystalline synths and driving rhythm
- Com Truise - "Flightwave" - Atmospheric dreamwave; floating pads and minimal rhythm
- Betamaxx - "Escapism" - Chillwave; smooth, meditative synthwave soundscape
- Gunship - "Dark All Day" - Retrowave with retro-futuristic storytelling and vocal elements
- Mitch Murder - "Interceptor" - Film score-influenced synthwave; orchestral and electronic blend
- Magic Sword - "Her Reverie" - Cinematic synthwave with dark, hypnotic qualities
