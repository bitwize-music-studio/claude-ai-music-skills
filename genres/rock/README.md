# Rock

## Genre Overview

Rock music is built on electric guitars, bass, drums, and vocals, with an emphasis on energy, attitude, and instrumental performance. From the jangly optimism of surf rock to the aggression of punk, rock spans decades of evolution while maintaining its core instrumentation and spirit.

## Characteristics

- **Instrumentation**: Electric guitar, bass guitar, drums, sometimes keys
- **Vocals**: Varies from melodic to aggressive, often prominent
- **Production**: Ranges from raw/lo-fi to polished studio sound
- **Energy**: Generally high-energy, performance-driven
- **Structure**: Verse-chorus, guitar solos, builds and releases

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Surf Rock | Jangly, reverb-drenched, 60s California | The Beach Boys, Dick Dale, Jan and Dean |
| Pub Rock | Working-class, straightforward, British | Dr. Feelgood, Ian Dury, Nick Lowe |
| Indie Rock | Alternative, DIY ethos, varied styles | Arcade Fire, The Strokes, Modest Mouse |
| Post-Punk | Angular, atmospheric, darker | Joy Division, The Cure, Interpol |
| Classic Rock | 70s-80s mainstream, arena-ready | Led Zeppelin, Queen, Fleetwood Mac |
| Punk Rock | Fast, aggressive, stripped-down | Ramones, The Clash, Sex Pistols |
