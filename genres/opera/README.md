# Opera

## Genre Overview

Opera combines theatrical drama with classical vocal technique and orchestral accompaniment. Characterized by powerful trained voices, dramatic storytelling, and full orchestration, opera ranges from tragic to comic, from classical to contemporary.

## Characteristics

- **Vocals**: Trained classical technique - vibrato, projection, dramatic phrasing
- **Instrumentation**: Full orchestra, sometimes piano accompaniment
- **Delivery**: Theatrical, emotive, sustained notes, coloratura runs
- **Structure**: Arias, recitatives, ensembles, overtures
- **Production**: Grand, orchestral, reverberant acoustic

## Subgenres & Styles

| Style | Description | Reference Artists |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Grand Opera | Large-scale, dramatic, full orchestra | Verdi, Puccini, Wagner |
| Comic Opera (Opera Buffa) | Comedic, lighter, often satirical | Mozart (Le Nozze di Figaro), Rossini |
| Operetta | Light opera, spoken dialogue, popular themes | Gilbert & Sullivan, Johann Strauss II |
| Contemporary Opera | Modern composition, experimental | Philip Glass, John Adams |
| Mock Opera | Parody, satirical, operatic delivery of comedic content | — |

For style reference when prompting:
- Puccini - "Nessun Dorma" (dramatic tenor aria)
- Mozart - "Queen of the Night" (coloratura soprano)
- Gilbert & Sullivan - "I Am the Very Model" (comic operetta)
- Bizet - "Habanera" from Carmen (mezzo-soprano)
- Verdi - "La donna è mobile" (tenor, dramatic)
