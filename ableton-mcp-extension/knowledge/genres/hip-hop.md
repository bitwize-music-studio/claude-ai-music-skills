# Hip-Hop Genre Guide

## Overview

Hip-hop is a cultural movement and music genre that emerged from the South Bronx, New York City in the early 1970s. Characterized by rhythmic vocal delivery (rapping), beat-driven production, and creative sampling, it has become one of the most influential cultural forces globally.

## Production Characteristics

### Instrumentation
- Drum machines (TR-808, MPC)
- Samplers and turntables
- Synthesizers and bass (sampled or synthesized)
- Occasional live instrumentation

### Vocals
- Rapping (rhythmic spoken/sung delivery)
- Sung hooks common
- Ad-libs and vocal layering
- Delivery ranges from smooth to aggressive

### Production Style
- Beat-driven with emphasis on drums and bass
- Heavy sampling tradition (soul, funk, jazz, rock)
- Modern production often synthesizer-based
- 808 bass dominant since late 2000s
- Sidechain compression and layered arrangements

### Tempo
- Boom-bap: 85-100 BPM
- Trap: 130-170 BPM (half-time feel at 65-85 BPM)
- Drill: 140-145 BPM

## Mixing Recommendations

### Stem Priorities
1. **Vocals** - Front and center (+1 dB gain)
2. **Bass/808** - Powerful sub presence (+1 dB gain)
3. **Drums** - Punchy and forward (+0.5 dB gain)
4. **Other elements** - Supporting role

### Frequency Focus
- **Sub-bass**: 25-60 Hz (preserve for 808s)
- **Bass weight**: 60-120 Hz
- **Kick punch**: 100-150 Hz
- **Vocal presence**: 2-4 kHz
- **Hi-hat sparkle**: 8-12 kHz

### Common Processing
- Vocal presence boost: +2.5 dB at 3 kHz
- Bass highpass: 25 Hz (keep sub)
- Aggressive vocal de-essing
- Tight drum compression

## Mastering Targets

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Target LUFS | -10 to -9 |
| True Peak | -1.0 dBTP |
| Compression Ratio | 2.0:1 to 2.5:1 |
| Low-end Mono | Below 120-140 Hz |
| Saturation | 0.2-0.25 drive |

## Subgenres

### Boom-Bap
- Classic East Coast sound
- Hard-hitting drums, prominent snare on 2 and 4
- Jazz/soul samples
- Lyrical complexity emphasized
- Reference: Nas, Wu-Tang Clan, A Tribe Called Quest

### Trap
- Southern-originated style
- 808 bass, rapid hi-hat rolls (triplet-based)
- Dark synthesizers, sparse arrangements
- Half-time feel creates hypnotic groove
- Reference: Future, Migos, Travis Scott

### Conscious/Political
- Socially aware content
- Production varies but often soul-influenced
- Emphasis on lyrical depth and message
- Reference: Kendrick Lamar, J. Cole, Common

### Lo-Fi Hip-Hop
- Chill, atmospheric production
- Jazz samples, vinyl crackle, muted drums
- Often instrumental
- Associated with study/relaxation playlists
- Reference: Nujabes, J Dilla, MF DOOM

### Drill
- Aggressive subgenre with dark production
- Sliding 808s and street narratives
- Regional variants: Chicago, UK, Brooklyn
- Reference: Chief Keef, Pop Smoke, Headie One

## Quality Checklist

### Mix Stage
- [ ] Vocals clear and present in the mix
- [ ] 808/bass doesn't muddy the low end
- [ ] Kick and bass work together (sidechain if needed)
- [ ] Hi-hats and percussion have clarity
- [ ] No frequency conflicts between elements

### Master Stage
- [ ] Target LUFS: -10 to -9
- [ ] True peak: -1.0 dBTP maximum
- [ ] Low end mono below 120-140 Hz
- [ ] Vocal presence preserved
- [ ] Competitive loudness without squashing transients

## Notes for Ableton Workflow

1. **Use stems when available** - Process vocals, drums, bass separately
2. **Sidechain bass to kick** - Essential for trap/modern hip-hop
3. **Parallel compression on drums** - Add punch without losing dynamics
4. **Vocal chain**: EQ → De-ess → Compress → Saturate → Delay/Reverb
5. **808 processing**: Highpass at 25Hz → Saturate → Compress → Limit
6. **Reference constantly** - A/B against commercial hip-hop releases
